Numbers in python

Sun 15 September 2019 | tags: Pythonintroductionnumbers

Disclaimer

What follows is not written to be 100% accurate in all the technical detail. It is written to help people with limited knowledge of computers to get started with using python. As such, I have tried to limit jargon so if you already know terms like "object" and "class", or what a command line is, then this page ain't for you. Feel free to stick around though.

You now know how to make strings like a boss, meaning that when it comes to typing the language you use in everyday life you have the basics under your belt. What we're moving onto now is something that comes to mind more often when people think of computers:

Numbers

Don't worry, we're not getting into complex maths, we're just going get used to playing with simple numbers in python.

Ints

The first way that python handles numbers is as the "int" type. This is basically just a whole number like 9 or 42(hehe) or 7000000000, the way you use numbers every day as you go through your life, doing numbery things... like counting "likes"... or what ever it is you people do.

Floats

The other way that python works with numbers is as floating-point numbers, of the "float" type. Just like ints, floats are pretty simple, they're just numbers followed by a decimal point. So 13.333333 is a float, and so is 0.2 etc. You know the deal.

Strings

Hopefully, you remember that when using normal langauge in python you use quotation marks to tell python what it is.

"Like this. Python knows that this is a string."

You can also, and hopefully this occured to you already, use numbers in your strings if you want to.

"Python also knows that this 2nd sentence is a string. I could put 3 numbers in this string if I wanted!"

This means you can write numbers into your sentences if you want to, but doing so this was means you can't do anything with those numbers. Yet! There is loads of ways you interact with strings and numbers that allow you to automate or update the numbers in a sentence, like when you need to update a report for example, and we'll get there. In the meantime just remember that there are ints, which are whole numbers, floats, numbers with decimal points, and strings, which is just using numberic symbols in normal language.

(Really) Simple math

So, when it comes to numbers, python will recognise a number, that isn't wrapped in quotes, as a number. This means you can just give python a number and every math instructions and python will give you back the result (again, we're taking it really slowly, you can do more than that). The usual math symbols, like + and - work just fine so lets just start there.

Et Voila! You've just done your first mathematical operation in python! Was it a small operation? Maybe, but you should be proud of yourself, it was the first of many. In the next post, we're going to cover more complex operations with numbers and strings by looking at the various 'operators', or symbols, you can use as instructions to tell python what to do.

Well done so far, keep up the good work!
